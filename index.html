Me

Hello! I'm Luke and I like to make things. I'm currently finishing my fourth year of Computer Science at the University of Bristol.

I specialise in cryptocurrency and have been working as a consultant in the field since mid-2014. I also tinker with web technologies, simulations, app development and generative art. I use C++, Python and Node day-to-day. I am a big advocate of privacy online, encryption and also open-source software. I try to contribute to the latter whenever possible and I encourage you to do the same!

When I make it outside I like to travel and I spent the summer cycling across Europe - and I've done so by hitch-hiking before! I'm also a keen walker (I prefer the term explorer!), a permaculturalist, an amateur photographer and a yogi (not to mention a lover of labels). I adore vegetables - seriously - and am a passionate vegetarian/vegan cook. Eating animals is one of the worst things you can do for the planet, try going without, it's great. Cryptocurrency

I have worked on several projects in the cryptocurrency space, creating bespoke currencies, advising on UI integration and helping businesses keep their coins secure. In 2014 I spent some time consulting for The Real Asset Co and assisting them in developing Goldbloc. I have also worked with the crowdfunding platform StartJOIN on a variety of web services (block explorers, web-wallets and paper wallet generators) and I am actively working with StartChat, an encrypted-messenger with an integrated Bitcoin wallet.

Human-Computer Interaction

During my undergraduate degree I spent a lot of time tinkering with computer interfaces. I built an arduino-based buzzer game called BuzzNote that provides musical feedback; I also built a magnetic stylus with haptic feedback.

Architecture

Another big use of time has been writing processor simulations. I have just completed a module called Advanced Computer Architecture, the coursework for which was to simulate a superscalar, out-of-order RISC processor. If you're interested, you can find this here.
